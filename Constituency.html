In Short:

  • Chandrababu Naidu counts government's welfare policies in Assembly
  • Talks about Super-Six schemes as promised during state elections
  • Says Rs 2 per kg rice, housing scheme for poor, women's safety on priority

Making it clear that welfare, development, and good governance are the policies of the Telugu Desam Party-led National Democratic Alliance (NDA), Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister N Chandrababu Naidu, on Wednesday told the Assembly that the state government will function with the sole aim of improving the living standard of the people.

"The state government is moving ahead by implementing the Super-Six schemes as promised in the election manifesto," the Chief Minister said, adding that it is the TDP that laid the foundation for welfare as the party founder, NT Rama Rao's slogan was everyone should have food to fill the stomach, clothes, and the necessary shelter.

The Rs 2 per kg rice scheme which was introduced by NTR is now being implemented across the country, and he also built houses for the poor with the aim of shifting them from the huts that they were living in then to pucca houses, Naidu recalled.

Naidu was addressing the Assembly on the completion of 162 days of the TDP-led NDA government in the state. Though elections and people's verdicts are nothing new, the awareness that the people have exhibited in the recent polls is really a history which he termed as a revolution.

"The people have given this verdict with confidence that the TDP-led NDA will certainly stand by them and will rise to their expectations. It is now our duty to rise to their expectations," the Chief Minister told the House.
